<h3>Overview</h3>

Designed and prototyped an assistive device to enable ALS patients with limited motor function to independently manage their gastrostomy tube feeding.

<div style="height: 1rem;"></div>

<div class="project-section" markdown="1">

<h3>Key Accomplishments</h3>

- Designed and prototyped a motorized pulley system with Arduino-based controls to raise/lower feeding bags for ALS patients with limited motor function and upper extremity weakness
- Engineered an accessible button interface requiring minimal range of motion, enabling independent feeding for patients with dysphagia
